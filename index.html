The Problem

Modern markets cannot reliably determine whether claims about the world are true.

Standards define rules, not reality

ISO and ISEAL tell us what should be true. They don't adjudicate what is true.

Audits don't survive pressure

Certification systems depend on the markets they verify. When challenged, they retreat behind "methodology."

Traceability isn't truth

Platforms track provenance but don't decide whether evidence is sufficient. If bad data enters cleanly, it persists cleanly.

Courts don't scale

Legal systems have legitimacy but are slow, expensive, and backward-looking. They enforce after collapse, not as part of the system.

"Truth without consequence is theater.
Consequence without truth is tyranny.
Veridion exists so neither outcome becomes inevitable."

What is Veridion?

Veridion is constitutional infrastructure for claim adjudication. It determines whether claims about the world—ESG performance, supply chain compliance, climate impact, community consent—are true in a way that survives time, power, and regime change.

  • ✓ Evidentiary thresholds that are transparent and contestable
  • ✓ Adjudicated outcomes that persist across time and vendors
  • ✓ Anti-capture governance by design
  • ✓ Separation from enforcement to preserve neutrality

Clear Boundaries

Veridion is precise about what it is—and what it is not.

Veridion is not

  • ✕ A regulator or enforcement body
  • ✕ A market operator or ratings agency
  • ✕ A blockchain oracle or token system
  • ✕ A software product or compliance vendor

Veridion is

  • ✓ Neutral truth infrastructure
  • ✓ Foundation-held, not corporate-owned
  • ✓ Designed to outlast its founders
  • ✓ Separated from enforcement by design
